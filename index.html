What's in the NSW Services Centre Driving Test?

Our most frequently asked questions are “What will I have to do in the driving test?” and “How long does it last?”

Knowing the main five criteria will help you prepare for the test:

Vehicle location
Car control
Manage speed
Perception of danger
Making choices


You must position the vehicle on the road for the Vehicle position section. This includes stopping at a safe distance from the vehicle in front (1-2 car lengths), keeping 50cm from the kerb during a reverse parallel park and kerb side stop, and maintaining good positioning through a corner or turn. Avoid driving in other cars' blind spots and be aware of your "safety cushion" around your car.

The testing officer wants to see you smoothly operate car controls and stay in control. Mirrors, seat belt, and seat position must be considered along with brakes, steering, clutch, gears, and accelerator.

The speed management section tests your ability to choose the right speed for the situation. Remember the three-second gap and consider vision, space around your car, other cars' speeds, and road surface.

You must demonstrate your ability to react quickly to road hazards in the hazard perception section. Your test officer will mark 5-10 hazards.

Decision making can be the hardest because you must always be aware of what's going on around the vehicle. You must proceed correctly at intersections and choose safe gaps. Entering a road or changing lanes requires judgement and experience.

Two of the following manoeuvres will be evaluated in addition to these five sections:

Kerbside stop
Hill start
Three-point turn
